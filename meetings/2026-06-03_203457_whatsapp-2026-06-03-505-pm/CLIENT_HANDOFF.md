## Content by body system (study guide)

### Neuro / general
- Use **nursing assessment and clinical judgment** (e.g. stroke suspicion)—not generic internet answers.
- **Remove all jewelry** early (including hidden piercings); metal is a concern if urgent **OR / cautery** is needed.

### Cardiovascular
- Check **pulses** while assessing the upper body.

### Respiratory
- Auscultate **front and back**; **sit the patient up**—large breasts can obscure lower lobes.
- Document **bilateral vs one-sided** findings (e.g. crackles on right only)—avoid vague “crackles” without side.
- Respiratory history: SOB, **smoking/vaping**, asthma, recent illness.
- **Hospital smoking:** O₂ risk; offer **nicotine patch/gum**; leaving to smoke may require **AMA**; manage withdrawal to keep patient on unit.

### Breasts
- Still assess in antepartum—ask about **implants / cosmetic surgery** (often not reported as “surgery”).
- **Modified breast exam** for lumps; many patients see OB more than PCP—cancer can be found in pregnancy.
- Implants can affect **breastfeeding** (placement above/below muscle).

### Abdomen / GI
- Bowel sounds **shift upward** when pregnant—listen in upper quadrants near fundus.
- **Hyperactive** sounds + diarrhea → hydration, **preterm labor** risk.
- Abdominal pain: distinguish **contractions** (intermittent) vs **constant** pain (appendicitis, gallbladder, etc.).
- **Reflux/chest pain** can mimic cardiac issues—clarify history and treat/document appropriately.

### GU / OB-specific
- **Clean catch urine:** wipe → void in toilet → catch sample.
- **UTI** can worsen pregnancy outcomes (including preterm labor).
- **Protein/creatinine (pre-eclampsia workup)** with vaginal bleeding: use **straight catheter**—clean catch with blood can cause **false elevated protein**.
- **PROM** (term) vs **PPROM** (preterm, membranes ruptured before labor).
- **Placenta previa / bleeding history:** **nothing in vagina** (no sex, careful exams).
- Assess **vaginal discharge** (color, odor, lesions)—infection/STI can trigger preterm labor or sepsis.
- **Rupture of membranes testing:** pooling, ferning; **RomPlus / Amnisure** and litmus can be **false positive** with blood or **KY jelly**—swab **before** digital exam when using litmus.

### Back / musculoskeletal
- **Back pain** in pregnancy can be **preterm labor** presentation—don’t dismiss as “normal pregnancy back pain” without context.
- Ask about **scoliosis, spinal surgery, trauma**—affects **epidural/spinal anesthesia** planning; escalate to anesthesia when appropriate.

### Extremities / skin
- Document **skin** findings (cellulitis, rashes, lesions)—baseline matters for “hospital-acquired” disputes.
- **Varicose veins (including vulvar)** → higher **DVT** risk; note for prophylaxis; rare severe bleeding with pushing.
- Check **pedal pulses**—compare **left and right at the same time**.
- Calf pain/redness: DVT concern.

### Psychosocial / discharge safety
- Even on short antepartum visits, ask: **safe at home**, support person, housing, resources, intimate partner safety.
- Example from session: homelessness discovered **near discharge** → **social work** and placement support.
