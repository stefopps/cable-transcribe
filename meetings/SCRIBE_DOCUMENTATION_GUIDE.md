## 1. Clarity & Writing

- **Be very clear; make notes look professional.** Think about who the readers are and pay attention to details. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Patients can read charts now** — clarity and concision matter beyond clinician-to-clinician communication. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Tailor writing to the intended reader's level of expertise.** Avoid abbreviations unfamiliar to non-medical staff. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Prioritize clarity over abbreviations** not widely recognized in medical terminology. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Use clear language** when documenting dates, times, observations, and lab results. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Ask questions and seek clarification** when unsure about documentation. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Clearly document all information**, even if not clearly dictated — but **do not assume normal findings** when not stated. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Be mindful of potential miscommunication** due to unclear documentation. *(2026-05-29)*
- **MDM is often written in past tense**, but it is case-dependent — ask if unsure. *(2026-05-27)*
- **Use shorthand on site** to keep up with dictations; training notes should match dictation exactly at first, then adapt to provider preferences. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Force yourself to use shorthand** over the next two weeks — that is how you keep up with clinicians. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Consistent, accurate documentation** is key to effective communication among providers. *(2026-05-28 finalize)*

## 2. Abbreviations

- **Use "PTA" sparingly, if at all**, to avoid confusion. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Do not use PT for "patient"** in notes — spell it out or clarify with clinicians. Clinicians may abbreviate when typing fast; scribes should fix this. *(2026-05-29)*
- **There is no official list** of allowed/disallowed medical terminology abbreviations — it comes with experience. Very few medication abbreviations are used. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Spell things out for the most part.** Use standard abbreviations when truly preferred on site (e.g., ETOH is common). *(2026-05-29, 2026-05-22)*
- **Vital sign abbreviations are acceptable in training** (T, BP, RR, RESB) — on site many populate automatically. O2 sat is an exception where formality matters. *(2026-05-22)*
- **Shorthand for common PE phrases:** VS (vital signs stable), RRR (regular rate and rhythm), LCTAB (lungs clear to auscultation bilaterally). *(2026-05-27)*

## 3. HPI (History of Present Illness)

- **The first sentence of the HPI usually tells a lot** — it typically captures the chief complaint. *(2026-05-28)*
- **You do not have to use the sentence structure built into the template** — add information the way it was dictated. *(2026-05-28)*
- If creating HPI after going in the room, you may use the template setup; if dictating, **stick to the way information was dictated**. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Do not combine sentences until you gain experience** — in the beginning: *"stick to not combining the sentences."* *(2026-05-28)*
- **Precipitating events** (e.g., dusty closet triggering asthma) are important; asthma templates mention precipitating factors. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Chronic condition history questions are common:** prior ED visits, admissions, intubation for asthma/COPD/CHF. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Pertinent negatives / "otherwise" section** goes in the HPI (e.g., "otherwise no fever, no chest pain…"). *(2026-05-27)*
- **If patient reports taking a medication**, include in HPI and **ask clinician** whether to add to medication list. *(2026-05-28)*
- **External records:** if you find relevant history, ask: *"Do you want me to add that to their history?"* *(2026-05-29)*
- **Surgical history in abdominal pain** helps establish differential (adhesions from prior C-sections, etc.). *(2026-05-28)*
- **Pain from sleep / early morning** is important context — do not confuse "no prior episodes" with "no prior episodes *like this*." *(2026-05-28)*
- **Cardiac risk factors** should be captured early in chest pain cases. *(2026-05-27)*

## 4. ROS / PMH / Medical History Consistency

- **Consistency is key:** if hypertension is in the HPI, it must also appear in medical history and align with the medication list. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Watch for contradictions:** chart says "no medical history on file" while HPI mentions HTN and med list shows 3 medications — report possible PMH to clinician. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Small details matter:** multiple meds or allergies on file suggest PMH even if not dictated — research and ask. *(2026-05-28)*
- **ROS vs HPI:** if clinician says "in the ROS capture this," put it in ROS; if in HPI dictation, it stays in HPI. *(2026-05-27)*
- Some providers prefer **"as per HPI"** in ROS — learn site/provider preference. *(2026-05-27)*
- **Avoid contradictions** between HPI and ROS. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Social history** (e.g., ETOH) can be free-texted; if unsure PMH vs social, ask doc/APP. *(2026-05-22)*

## 5. Physical Exam (PE)

- **On site, clinicians do not label findings** ("cardiovascular:", "neuro:") — scribes must figure out where things go. **Ask if unsure.** *(2026-05-29)*
- **Do not change positive findings to negative** just because something was not re-dictated (e.g., do not flip positive distal pulses to negative). *(2026-05-29)*
- **If a finding was not assessed, leave the template default** — do not document gait if patient would not walk; do not document heel-to-shin if not assessed. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Neuro section overlaps most frequently** — ask when uncertain. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Skin findings:** if unsure skin vs another section, ask. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Do not guess distress level** — always ask clinician and select from smart list. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Positive findings can go at beginning or end** of a PE subsection. *(2026-05-22)*
- **Manipulate templates freely** — pull PE from another template if clinician requests. *(2026-05-22)*
- **Memorize common template PE defaults** so you know when "normal" dictation means you can skip writing everything. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Awake, alert, no acute distress (A&O, NAD)** — for mild distress use "MAD." *(2026-05-27)*

## 6. Diagnostics

### Section placement

- Results go in the **diagnostic section** (after ED course/MDM header). *(2026-05-29, 2026-05-22)*
- **EKG, portable chest X-ray, head CT, labs** belong in diagnostics; rhythm strip reading goes in **Proc doc**. *(2026-05-29)*
- **Total critical care time** also goes in Proc doc — required for coding/billing. *(2026-05-29, 2026-05-28)*
- **Official radiologist/pathologist results** go at top of diagnostic section with timestamps. *(2026-05-27 finalize)*
- **Lab discussion/summary** can go in MDM; official results stay in diagnostics. *(2026-05-27)*

### EKG / rhythm strip

- After EKG, **always ask:** *"What should I put for the rhythm strip?"* *(2026-05-22)*
- Use **"as interpreted by me"** (or named interpreter) for ED clinician reads. *(2026-05-22)*
- Normal axis/intervals do not need +/- signs; ST elevations, infiltrates, etc. use +/- notation. *(2026-05-22)*
- **Rhythm strip must be in Proc doc** or it cannot be coded/billed correctly. *(2026-05-27, 2026-05-22)*

### X-ray / CT / ultrasound

- **Always capture ED clinician interpretation** — even if radiologist read returns first. *(2026-05-22)*
- If radiology read is back and clinician has not interpreted: *"Chest X-ray was read by radiology and it's normal — what do you want me to put for your interpretation?"* *(2026-05-22)*
- **Wet read:** if provider enters wet read, it populates — delete duplicate free-text and refresh. *(2026-05-21)*
- X-ray documentation requires: **(1) body part**, **(2) result/interpretation**, **(3) "as interpreted by me"**. *(2026-05-22)*
- **Do not assume** what an X-ray is of — must state explicitly (left ankle, etc.). *(2026-05-22)*

### Labs

- In training: **copy/paste labs from chat**; on site smart text pulls from lab system. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Refresh smart link** for labs — you do not manually add each lab on site. *(2026-05-22)*
- If you miss dictated lab values, **go to the labs** to find them. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Do not interpret labs** — capture what clinician says (high, low, etc.). *(2026-05-22)*

### Proactive tracking

- **Stay organized:** track pending labs, imaging, EKG interpretations. *(2026-05-22)*
- *"If you're sitting at your computer for a 12-hour shift and you haven't gotten up, you're not doing the job right."* *(2026-05-22)*
- Get up, follow results, ask for interpretations, learn who techs/radiology/nurses are. *(2026-05-22)*

## 7. MDM (Medical Decision-Making)

- **Split shared services attribution:**
  - **APP alone** → APP's name for MDM
  - **MDM** (physician involved) → doctor's name *(2026-05-28)*
  - Say **"as interpreted by Dr. [Name]"** when physician interprets imaging/labs on split-shared cases. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Ask for interpretation at borderline values** (~94% O2 sat): *"Is this adequate, borderline, or inadequate?"* *(2026-05-28)*
- **Prescription management** includes instructions, dose changes, frequency changes, discontinuations. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Follow dictation order** for labs/imaging/reassessment even if template order differs. *(2026-05-27)*
- **You can move, add, or delete smart phrase sections** based on what applies. *(2026-05-27)*

## 8. Critical Care

### Triggers

- Critical care = severe illness/injury with **high probability of imminent or life-threatening deterioration**. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Keywords:** severe respiratory distress, stroke symptoms, severe abdominal pain, unstable vitals, critical lab values, radiologist calling with brain bleed. *(2026-05-29, 2026-05-28)*

### Time & coding

- **Minimum 30 minutes total critical care time** (not consecutive). *(2026-05-28)*
- **Ask clinician:** *"How much critical care time do you want?"* — they calculate total. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Total critical care minutes must go in Proc doc** for coding/billing/MIPS. *(2026-05-28)*
- **PPE donning/doffing counts** toward critical care time. *(2026-05-28)*

### Workflow

- Choose **critical care workflow** from **EDM-DM smart list**. *(2026-05-29)*
- Use **"critical care called"** smart phrase (replaces older OBS dot phrases). *(2026-05-28)*
- **Fluid resuscitation** (not "hydration") reflects intensity of critical care therapy. *(2026-05-28, 2026-05-29)*
- **Do not use the "code" button** — type medical terms and free-text if needed. *(2026-05-29)*

### Anti-patterns

- **Avoid documenting "stable" or "no distress"** throughout a critical care note. *(2026-05-28)*
- Use **"resuscitation"** language, not casual "hydration." *(2026-05-28)*
- **Critical care and observation cannot be coded same calendar day.** *(2026-05-28)*

## 9. Observation

- **Ask proactively:** *"Are any of these patients appropriate for observation or critical care?"* *(2026-05-27)*
- Choose **observation workflow** from EDM-DM smart list when clinician says OBS or order exists. *(2026-05-27)*
- **Minimum 2 hours** observation time for OBS coding. *(2026-05-27)*
- **Fill out ALL observation prompts** — reassessments, labs/imaging, disposition. *(2026-05-27)*
- **Put reassessment timestamps BEFORE the sentence** — military time. *(2026-05-28, 2026-05-27)*
- **Do not use quick-button/outdated OBS templates** — use **`RWJBH ED [condition]`** templates. *(2026-05-27)*
- **Anaphylactic shock = critical care**, not observation. *(2026-05-27)*

## 10. Workflows & Templates

- **Always search `RWJBH ED [condition]`** — speed buttons hold ~90% updated templates. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Push providers toward system-wide RWJ templates** — billing, coding, MIPS, medicolegal requirements built in. *(2026-05-21)*
- RDU templates: search **`RWJBH RDU`**. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Wildcards (`***`) and smart lists (pink boxes)** must be addressed before note can be signed. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Scribe smart phrase + attestation** required on every note. *(2026-05-22, 2026-05-28)*
- Change attestation from placeholder to **actual APP or physician name**. *(2026-05-22)*

---

## 11. MIPS Measures

- **Use RWJ templates** — MIPS measures built in; pull dot phrases if template lacks a measure. *(2026-05-27)*
- Ask: *"What do you want me to put for the MIPS measure?"* *(2026-05-27)*
- Key measures: sepsis, MOUD/opioid, back pain, DVT, head CT/PECARN, CVA/thrombolytics, smoking cessation, URI/sinusitis. *(2026-05-27)*

## 12. Timeliness & Submission

- Submit notes by **4:20–4:30 PM** to avoid late penalties. *(2026-05-27, 2026-05-28)*
- Send notes in **even if not complete** by deadline — partial beats missing. *(2026-05-27)*
- **Proofread charts before sending** — errors and HIPAA issues; check prescriptions/spelling. *(2026-05-27, 2026-05-28)*

## 13. Epic Tips

- Customize **tracking grid** — remove unused columns. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Write down MRNs** so patients do not disappear from tracking grid. *(2026-05-21)*
- Notes from **clinician perspective**, not triage nurse. *(2026-05-21)*
- **Save work before leaving computer** during long critical care cases. *(2026-05-28)*

## 14. Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

| Anti-pattern | Why | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Using PT for patient | Patients read charts; unclear | 2026-05-29 |
| Overusing PTA | Confusing | 2026-05-29 |
| Combining HPI sentences early in training | Loses dictated structure | 2026-05-28 |
| Changing PE positives to negatives when not re-dictated | Inaccurate | 2026-05-29 |
| Assuming normal/assessed findings not dictated | Inaccurate | 2026-05-29 |
| Guessing distress level | Ask clinician | 2026-05-21 |
| **Never assume** — put wrong info in chart | Ask instead | 2026-05-22 |
| Leaving observation prompts blank | Clinician deletes note; no OBS coding | 2026-05-27 |
| Documenting "stable/no distress" in critical care | QA flag | 2026-05-28 |
| Forgetting rhythm strip or CC time in Proc doc | Cannot code/bill | 2026-05-29 |
| Sitting at desk all shift | Miss results, interpretations | 2026-05-22 |
| Changing lane diagnoses yourself | Recognize and flag only | 2026-05-28 |

## 15. Lane Diagnoses (LANE = Low Acuity Non-Emergent)

- **Non-specific diagnoses** that do not fully capture care/risk: generic pain, cough/congestation/viral syndrome, sprain/strain, dizziness, epistaxis. *(2026-05-28)*
- Many end in **"-itis"** — think: is this a lane diagnosis? *(2026-05-28)*
- **Your job: recognize and bring to clinician's attention** — do NOT change the diagnosis unless asked. *(2026-05-28)*
- **Be especially alert when scribing for APPs.** *(2026-05-28)*
